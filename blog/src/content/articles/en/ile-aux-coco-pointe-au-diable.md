**Île aux Coco** is probably the most spectacular natural site in the Rodrigues lagoon. It is a protected nature reserve, a sanctuary for seabirds (mainly terns) — access is controlled and limited, which preserves its magic.

## A postcard beach

The island is nothing but a long strip of white sand barely rising out of the water. No vegetation, no buildings — just the sand, the birds and the blue of the lagoon. The visual effect is striking.

## Pointe au Diable

The outing is usually combined with **Pointe au Diable**, another remarkable spot in the north-west. Depending on the operator, lunch may be served on one of the islets.

## Practical info

- **Duration:** full day (morning departure, late-afternoon return)
- **Regulated access:** booking with a licensed operator is mandatory
- **Bring along:** swimsuit, towel, extra-strong sun protection (zero shade), hat, plenty of water
- **Good to know:** the tide affects the crossing — the operator sets the schedule
